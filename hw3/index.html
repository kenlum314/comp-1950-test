Homework #2: Places I Have Visited and HTML5 Features

Places I Have Visited

Here are two places I have visited: Edmonton, Alberta and San Francisco, California. I lived in the former for three months back in 2002 for a summer job, while the latter I visited four times between 1990 and 2014.

In the brief profiles below, I will compare each city to Vancouver:

Edmonton, Alberta

Edmonton is a relatively modern city, with some new neighbourhoods entirely comprised of new houses, something not found in Vancouver. One of its major tourist attractions is the West Edmonton Mall (one of the largest malls in the world). Aside from the latter, I wasn't able to explore other tourist attractions as I was there to work:-(

My favourite part about Edmonton is its roads. They are wide and afford drivers great visibility. Where we see stop signs in Metro Vancouver, Edmonton has yield signs, so there is no legal requirement to come to a complete stop. Truth be told, everyone in Vancouver treats the stop signs as yield signs anyhow; but in Edmonton, it is legal to do so!

San Francisco, California

With relatives in San Francisco, I visited the city several times as a tourist. San Francisco is older than Vancouver and has very hilly terrain. A notable characteristic of San Francisco lies in its residential neighbourhoods, which primarily consist of row houses—houses which are in physical contact with each other along their adjacent walls.

My favourite part about San Francisco is that its climate reminds me of what I wished Vancouver's would be. The weather is essentially a warmer, foggier, and less rainy version of Vancouver weather.


HTML5 Features

Two new HTML5 tags I have used on this page that were not demonstrated in class are the time and summary tags.

The time Tag

  1. The time tag surrounds the years listed in the introduction of the previous section (invoke your browser's element inspector on the years to see the markup). The purpose of the time tag is to semantically mark content as human-readable temporal information.
  2. The time tag has one specific attribute, datetime, which is used to define machine-readable temporal information (e.g. <time datetime="2015-03-14 00:00">Start of Pi Day 2015</time>).
  3. Browser Compatibility (latest versions) IE Firefox Chrome Safari Opera
    Compatible? Yes. Yes. Yes. Yes. Yes.

  4. Remarks: The time tag currently isn't rendered in a manner that makes it visually distinct from its surrounding text.

The summary Tag

  1. The summary tag is used to give a title to (or summarize) the contents of the details tag. The summary tag must be used as a child of the details tag (and should be the first child tag). A summary tag can be seen at the bottom of this page, providing a title to the hidden assignment details.
  2. The summary tag has no specific attributes.
  3. Browser Compatibility (latest versions) IE Firefox Chrome Safari Opera
    Compatible? No. No. Yes. Yes. Yes.

  4. Remark A: Without the HTML5 Shiv, the details and summary tags, along with all other HTML5-specific tags, do not get styled properly in IE 8 and below. With the Shiv, the details and summary tags still don't function properly in those versions of IE, but at least they style reasonably.

    Remark B: In the browsers that are not compatible with the details and summary tags, the accordion widget is not present, but all the content (including the normally hidden content) is still visible and readable.

Note: The details tag is meant to give additional information in an accordion-like widget that can be opened or closed by the user.
